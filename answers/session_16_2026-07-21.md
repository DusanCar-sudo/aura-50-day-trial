## Why `EPSILON_PROBE_RATE` is kept low (0.05)

The epsilon probe exists to solve a **score freeze** problem: when the competence gate decides `useArchimedes: false` for a pattern (because its success rate dropped below threshold), that pattern's `archimedesAttempted` flag never becomes `true` again — so no new episode is recorded, and the score is **permanently stuck** at a low value, even if the underlying local model later improves.

The probe is an epsilon-greedy override: with probability `EPSILON_PROBE_RATE` (0.05 = 5%), it flips `useArchimedes` back to `true` even when the gate said no, forcing a fresh trial that generates new evidence.

### Cost implications

The rate is kept low because **a probe is not free** — it's not a lightweight background check. Every probe trial still pays the **full cost chain**:

1. **Archimedes (local model) runs** — consumes CPU/Ollama resources
2. **The large model verifies the result** — verification always runs regardless of which model attempted the task
3. **If Archimedes fails, the large model re-does the work** — the probe may actually increase total cost by running both models on the same question

So at 0.05, roughly 1 in 20 gated tasks triggers a probe. That's enough to keep scores "live" and recoverable (a model improvement will be detected within ~20 trials), without inflating cost by running unnecessary full-cost probes on every gated task. The constant is also **configurable per project** via `archimedes.epsilonProbeRate` in `.aura.json`, so projects with tighter cost budgets can lower it further, and those needing faster recovery can raise it.[cache] hit=8832 miss=393 input=9225
